- **Policy makers** drafting legislation (e.g., EV subsidies, carbon taxes)

- Wasted public funds (avg. policy failure rate: ~60%)

- Delayed course-correction (years to see real-world feedback)

## 💡 SOLUTION OVERVIEW

### What SYNAPSE GRID Does
A **multi-domain decision simulation platform** where users:
1. Describe a real-world scenario in plain language
2. Tune parameters via sliders (budget, scale, timeframe)
3. Run simulation → get outcomes in seconds
4. Compare multiple scenarios side-by-side
5. Export and share a "Future Snapshot" card

## ❓ JUDGE Q&A PREP

**Q: How is the simulation accurate?**
A: "The simulation engine uses calibrated elasticity models from peer-reviewed energy economics research (NBER, IEA data). It's not a random number generator — it's a rule-based system that mirrors known economic relationships. As we collect real-world outcome data, we'll train ML models to replace the rules engine."

**Q: How is this different from just using ChatGPT?**
A: "ChatGPT gives you text. SYNAPSE GRID gives you structured, comparable, parameterized outcomes with visual dashboards and comparison mode. It's domain-specific, auditable, and reproducible. You can't compare two ChatGPT answers side-by-side with delta charts."

**Q: What's your data source?**
A: "The base parameters come from IEA World Energy Outlook, EPA emissions data, NREL EV adoption research, and Bureau of Labor Statistics job multiplier studies. The simulation weights are calibrated against historical policy outcomes."

**Q: Why haven't big companies built this?**
A: "They have — it's called McKinsey and costs $500K. We're democratizing it."

**Q: What's the moat?**
A: "Three things: the domain-specific rule engine calibrated from real data, the scenario comparison UX (nobody does this well), and the network effect — every shared Future Snapshot brings new users who add new scenarios that improve the engine."
